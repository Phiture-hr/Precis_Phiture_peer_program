Executive Summary

The Bridge Program pairs 60 people, 30 from Phiture and 30 from Precis, for a 12-week peer exchange. You get matched with one person from the other company. Over 12 weeks you have 5 structured sessions: you teach them something you are genuinely good at, they teach you something you actually want to learn. That is it. No hierarchy, no mentoring, no forced networking.

Program Concept

What it is

You get matched with one person from the other company. Over 12 weeks, you have 5 structured sessions together. You teach them something you are genuinely good at, they teach you something you actually want to learn. You both show up, you both prepare, you both get something out of it. Neither person is the expert, neither is the student.

What this is not This is not mentoring. There is no mentor and no mentee. It is not networking, there are no coffees disguised as development. Every session has a learning goal set in advance and a short reflection written afterwards. If a session cannot be described in terms of what was taught and what was learned, it does not count.

Why this exists

Phiture and Precis are now one company, but most people on both sides have never actually worked with someone from the other. The Bridge Program is how that changes. You have expertise that Precis colleagues do not have access to. They have expertise you do not. This is a structured way to make that exchange happen, rather than waiting for it to happen by accident.

How it works for you

  • You teach something real. Not a generic skill. Something specific you have actually used in your work in the past 6 months, specific enough to teach in 45 minutes.
  • You learn something you actually want. You name the gap when you apply. We match you with someone who can genuinely help close it.
  • You own your schedule. You and your pair decide when you meet. There is a suggested biweekly cadence, but no one is checking your calendar.
  • The Festival is a bonus. By September, you and your pair will have been working together for months. Meeting in person at the Festival is a natural next step, not a first impression.
2

How it works, 1:1 Pairs

30 pairs, one Phiture and one Precis per pair. Five sessions of 60 minutes over 12 weeks, alternating who leads. Both people prepare before each session. Both write a short reflection after. That is the full structure, everything else is up to you and your pair.

Why 1:1 works better than a group

  • You cannot be passive. In a 1:1 there is nowhere to hide. You both have to show up. In a group, it is easy to let others carry the session.
  • The conversation goes deeper. A 1:1 naturally moves towards the specific, the candid, the useful. Groups stay more general.
  • You build an actual relationship. After 5 sessions with one person, you know them. After 5 group sessions with four people, you kind of know four people.

How it works in practice

When you are matched, you both receive a shared Google Doc with a simple template for each of your 5 sessions. Before each session, whoever is leading that one writes a one-sentence learning objective in the doc. After the session, whoever was being taught writes three sentences: what I learned, how I will use it, what I still want to understand.

You and your pair agree your own schedule. There is a suggested biweekly rhythm, but no one is tracking it. The only thing that matters is that all 5 sessions happen before the Festival.

Sessions alternate direction. You lead sessions 1, 3, and 5. Your pair leads 2 and 4. Leading a session means: you pick the topic, you prepare something to share, you are responsible for making that hour worth your pair's time.

What the shared document looks like

Each pair's document has 5 session sections, each with:

  • Date & who led, filled in before the call
  • Learning objective, one sentence written by the session leader the day before
  • Session notes, freeform, written during or immediately after
  • 3-sentence reflection, written by the person who was taught: what I learned, how I will use it, what I still don't understand

The document is private to the pair. The coordinator only sees the Session 5 summary, which both people submit at the end of the programme.

Participant Selection

Cohort composition

Cohort 1 targets 60 participants: 30 from Phiture (27% of the company) and 30 from Precis (~6% of the company). This asymmetry is intentional, Phiture's broad representation matters for cultural integration, while Precis participation must be filtered for relevance and motivation. Not all of Precis should be in this programme.

Eligibility, both companies

  • Minimum 6 months in current role, settled enough to have something concrete to teach
  • No planned parental leave or extended leave during the active programme window (July–September)
  • Manager approval, not to gatekeep, but to confirm the workload supports the commitment
  • Minimum realistic commitment of 3 hours per month (sessions + prep + reflection)

Eligibility, Precis only

  • Works in a function with strategic overlap with Phiture: Performance Marketing, Data & Analytics, Strategy, Creative, Product, or People
  • Has expressed interest in mobile app marketing, ASO, or growth marketing in the past 12 months, OR has a current or potential cross-sell relationship with Phiture clients
  • Manager level or above, OR a specialist with 3+ years in their domain (to ensure genuine expertise to offer)
  • Nominated by department head or self-nominated with department head endorsement, not open to unsolicited applications

Selection scoring, panel of two reviewers

CriterionWeightHow assessed
Specificity of skill to teach30%Q1, is the skill named precisely and demonstrated recently? Reject if vague.
Genuine learning motivation25%Q2 + Q5, is the gap real and not already addressable internally?
Integration relevance25%Q4 + Q7 (Precis), does the role or interest actually bridge both companies?
Reciprocal mindset20%Q3, evidence of learning from people with different backgrounds; not a one-way teaching mindset.
Automatic rejection triggers Reject if: the skill in Q1 is too broad to teach in a 45-minute session (e.g. "communication", "strategy", "leadership") · the applicant cannot name a specific learning gap · Precis applicant has no functional overlap with Phiture · the answer to Q3 describes mentoring someone junior, not learning from a peer.

Application Form

The application takes 15–20 minutes to complete. It is not a formality, the quality of answers directly determines who gets selected and how well they are matched. Applicants should know this upfront. The form is sent as a Google Form or Typeform link in the launch communication, with a 2-week submission window.

Instruction shown at the top of the form "This form takes 15–20 minutes. Your answers determine whether you are selected and, if you are, who you are matched with. Vague answers lead to weak matches. Be specific, the more precisely you describe your skill and your learning goal, the better your experience will be."

Matching Logic

Input, Matching Profile

After selection, each participant completes a short Matching Profile (5 fields, 10 minutes):

  • Primary skill to teach, pulled from Q1 of the application; participant confirms or refines
  • Primary skill to learn, pulled from Q3; participant confirms or refines
  • Secondary skill to teach, from Q2 if provided
  • Function and team, for diversity scoring
  • Preferred working style, choose: structured/flexible; async-friendly/sync-preferred

Matching criteria and weights

Criterion 1, A's skill to teach matches B's skill to learn40%
Criterion 2, B's skill to teach matches A's skill to learn (reverse direction)30%
Criterion 3, Functional diversity (different teams/domains within Phiture × Precis)30%

Scoring is 0–5 per criterion. The weighted total gives a match score out of 5.0. The coordinator builds a simple scoring matrix in a Google Sheet, reviews the top 3 candidates per person, and makes the final call manually.

Manual override rules

  • Client overlap. If A and B already work on the same client account, do not pair them, they have a professional relationship that will distort the exchange.
  • Seniority gap. Avoid very senior Precis + very junior Phiture (and vice versa). A large seniority gap recreates a mentoring dynamic even when neither person intends it.
  • Working style mismatch. If one person is strongly async and the other strongly sync, flag but do not reject, note it in both welcome messages so the pair can address it in Session 1.

Quality threshold

Minimum standards 90% of pairs should score ≥3.0 out of 5.0 overall. No pair should be made if Criterion 1 alone scores below 2.0, if the core teaching direction does not work, the programme does not work. Use the waitlist to find a better match rather than forcing a poor one.

Pre-Festival Activation

The week before the Festival, each pair completes a short preparation exercise. This is not admin, it is the thing that makes the first in-person meeting feel meaningful rather than awkward.

The joint brief, 1 page, 3 prompts

Each pair co-writes a single short document before they travel to the Festival. The template has three prompts:

  1. What I learned from my partner, 2–3 specific points, not a summary. Not "I learned about ASO" but "I learned how to use search tab impression share to identify ranking ceilings before committing to a metadata update."
  2. How I have used or plan to use it, one concrete example from their own work or a client context, even if it is still a plan rather than something already done.
  3. One question I still have, something they could not resolve in the sessions and want to explore when they meet in person. This becomes the opening for the first conversation at the Festival.
Why this matters Pairs who arrive at the Festival with a specific open question have a real conversation. Pairs who arrive without one end up asking "so how has it been going?" and standing next to each other for 10 minutes. The brief creates the hook.

The document is private to the pair, it is not shared with the coordinator or anyone else. It is simply the conversation starter for when they meet. Pairs have 5 working days to complete it after the template is sent on 1 September.

Festival Meetup

By the time the Precis Festival comes around in September 2026, you and your pair will have spent 5 hours working through real things together. The Festival is a great occasion to finally meet in person. It is not the end of the programme, it is just a moment within it, and probably the most fun one.

What this will feel like You will already know this person. You will have taught each other something, worked through a real problem together, and had at least a few good conversations. Meeting them at the Festival will feel like catching up with a colleague, not introducing yourself to a stranger.

What to expect at the Festival

  • There is no Bridge Program session at the Festival. No stage moment, no presentation, no structured activity.
  • Bridge participants will have a small indicator on their badge so you can spot your pair (and other Bridge colleagues) easily in the crowd.
  • Find your pair, grab a drink, pick up where you left off. That is it.
  • If you have not yet finished all your sessions, the Festival is a great time to talk about what is left, and to book your last one.

After the Festival

Whether the programme continues is up to you. Some pairs will keep meeting. Some will just stay in touch. Either is fine. The coordinator will share a link to the alumni Slack channel where you can stay connected with the wider group, and there will be an optional Alumni Hour in November for anyone who wants to reconnect.

9

Post-Program Continuation

Alumni track, October to December

  • All 60 participants join #bridge-alumni on Slack, the coordinator posts one prompt per month (e.g. "What is one thing your pair taught you that you have now passed on to someone on your team?")
  • One optional Alumni Hour in November, open format, no agenda, 60 minutes for reconnection and peer sharing across all pairs
  • Pairs who want to continue meeting 1:1 are encouraged but not tracked, it becomes self-sustaining at this point

Cohort 2 pipeline

In December, a brief expression-of-interest form goes out for Cohort 2 (targeting Q2 2027). Alumni from Cohort 1 are invited to become peer ambassadors, they help promote the programme and can optionally be paired with a new participant as a companion, not as a re-run of the full programme.

Institutional knowledge

The Session 5 joint summaries and end-of-programme survey data are compiled into a Cohort 1 Impact Report, a one-page document shared with leadership from both companies. This is the primary input for the Cohort 2 decision.

Communication Strategy

Positioning, lead with skill, not culture Do not position this as an HR initiative or an integration exercise. Position it as professional development with competitive value: you learn something specific that makes you better at your job. The headline is "Build a skill. Build a relationship. Build the company.", in that order.

What to avoid

  • Never use the word "mentoring", it creates the wrong expectation immediately
  • Do not frame it as a culture or DEI initiative, lead with skill development every time
  • Maximum one reminder per phase, do not spam; it signals desperation and undermines the programme's perceived exclusivity
  • Do not over-promise outcomes, be specific about what the programme delivers and what it does not
