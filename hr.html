Bridge Program
Coordinator Guide

Scoring criteria, matching logic, communications plan, success metrics, and the coordinator week-by-week checklist. Do not share this URL with participants.

1

Application scoring criteria

Each application is scored out of 100 points across five criteria. Score responses after the window closes (30 May) and before the matching meeting (7 June). Two reviewers score independently and average their scores.

Key principle Score based on specificity and self-awareness, not on seniority or writing polish. A mid-level employee who clearly names a skill gap scores higher than a senior person who is vague.
Criterion Weight What strong looks like (4–5 pts) What weak looks like (1–2 pts)
Skill specificity (Q1)
Can they teach something real and named?		 30 pts Names a distinct, practiced skill with a concrete recent example. Not a job title, a specific method or capability. "Communication", "strategy", or a job title. No example of actual use in the past 6 months.
Learning urgency (Q3)
Is their gap real and currently unmet?		 25 pts Describes a specific gap they cannot fill internally. Explains why their current team doesn't cover this. Generic aspiration ("I'd love to learn more about digital marketing"). No explanation of why it's unmet.
Mindset and self-awareness (Q4)
Can they learn across difference?		 20 pts Reflects honestly on what was hard, not just what worked. Shows capacity to learn from uncomfortable feedback. A positive anecdote only. No acknowledgement of difficulty or personal blind spots.
Integration relevance (Q6)
Will this pairing serve the integration?		 15 pts Identifies a plausible connection between their role and the integration, even indirectly. Shows curiosity about how the other company works. Ignores the integration angle or says "not relevant to my role" without exploring any connection.
Commitment (Q5 + overall tone)
Will they follow through?		 10 pts Selects 4h+ and the overall application reads as deliberate, not hasty. Answers are proportionate in length to the question. Selects 2h minimum and answers are very short across the board, suggesting low investment.

2

Matching logic

Matching happens in the week of 8 June. Target 12–20 pairs for the first cohort. Use the scoring sheet in Google Sheets combined with the matching rules below. The coordinator makes final decisions, not an algorithm.

Matching rules (apply in this order)

  • Rule 1, Non-negotiable: cross-company pairing. Every pair must be one Phiture person and one Precis person. No same-company pairs.
  • Rule 2: Skill complementarity. Person A's Q1 (teach) should match Person B's Q3 (learn), and vice versa. This is the primary matching signal. A partial match on one direction is acceptable; a zero match on both is not.
  • Rule 3: Avoid same seniority deadlock. The programme works better when there is a slight asymmetry. Two people who both teach the same depth may not challenge each other enough. Prefer pairing people whose skills are in different domains, even if seniority is similar.
  • Rule 4: Avoid functional silos. Do not pair two people from identical functions (e.g. both ASO leads). The point is cross-pollination. Same function can work only if their named skills are genuinely different.
  • Rule 5: Commitment match. If one person selected 2h and the other 5h+, flag the pair. A significant mismatch in commitment creates tension. Try to match within one band.
  • Rule 6: Time zone proximity. Where possible, pair people within 3 hours of each other. Bridge pairs that span large time zones have lower completion rates. Note any cross-zone pairings for the kickoff comms.

Skill complementarity matrix (use as a guide, not a rulebook)

This is a simplified guide to cross-company skill pairings that are known to generate high-value exchanges. Green = strong fit, Yellow = workable, — = avoid.

Mobile CRM / lifecycle ✓ Strong Cross-channel measurement ✓ Strong
App monetisation strategy ✓ Strong Brand strategy / positioning ✓ Strong
Retention / push analytics ~ Workable Programmatic buying ~ Workable
Creative testing frameworks ~ Workable SEO at scale ~ Workable
Generic project management — Avoid Generic leadership skills — Avoid
What to do with unmatched applicants Keep a waitlist. If a pair drops out in the first 4 weeks, pull from the waitlist and introduce the replacement as a "late joiner." After week 4, offer the unmatched person a place in the next cohort, with a personal note and priority consideration.

4

Success metrics

These are the metrics that determine whether to continue, scale, or redesign the programme. Measure them at two points: week 6 (mid-programme check-in) and end of September (close of programme).

Session completion rate
≥ 80%
Percentage of scheduled sessions that actually happen across all pairs. Tracked via self-reported log.
Pair retention to end
≥ 75%
Percentage of matched pairs still active at the end of the programme (vs. dropped before session 3).
Perceived skill gain
≥ 7 / 10
End-of-programme survey. "On a scale of 1–10, how much did you grow the skill you identified in Q3?" Average score across all respondents.
Programme NPS
≥ +30
Net Promoter Score from end survey: "How likely are you to recommend this programme to a colleague?" (0–10 scale). NPS = % Promoters minus % Detractors.
Application reapply rate
≥ 60%
Percentage of participants who say they would apply again for a second cohort. Measured in end survey.
Cross-company connections
Qualitative
Track whether pairs continue to collaborate informally after the programme ends. Qualitative only, captured via follow-up DMs at month 3 post-programme.

Decision thresholds

Use these to frame the October retrospective decision.

Scenario
Signals
Recommended action
Scale
Session rate ≥ 80%, NPS ≥ +30, reapply ≥ 60%
Run a larger cohort (25–35 pairs) in Q1 2027. Open applications wider.
Iterate
Mixed results: 2 of 3 primary metrics met
Run second cohort same size. Address the one failing metric with a specific fix (e.g. stricter matching, better kickoff, mid-check-in earlier).
Redesign

Tech setup: Google Apps Script

The application form (apply.html) posts directly to a Google Sheet via Google Apps Script. There is no backend server or database. Setup takes around 10 minutes.

What you need A Google account with access to Google Sheets and Apps Script. The Google Sheet should be in a shared People & Culture Drive folder, not a personal Drive.

Step-by-step setup

  • Step 1. Create a new Google Sheet. Rename the default tab to Responses. In row 1, add these headers exactly (one per column): Timestamp, Name, Email, Company, Role, Department, Q1_Skill_Teach, Q2_Skill_Teach_2, Q3_Skill_Learn, Q4_Mindset, Q5_Hours, Q6_Integration, Q7_Precis_Only
  • Step 2. In the Sheet, go to Extensions → Apps Script. Delete any existing code and paste the script below.
  • Step 3. Click Deploy → New deployment. Choose Web app. Set "Execute as" to Me. Set "Who has access" to Anyone (this allows the form to post without login). Click Deploy. Copy the Web App URL.
  • Step 4. Open apply.html in a text editor. Find the line const SCRIPT_URL = 'YOUR_SCRIPT_URL_HERE'; near the bottom and replace the placeholder with your copied URL. Save and re-upload to GitHub.
  • Step 5. Test: fill in the form and submit. Check the Sheet — a new row should appear with your test data. If it doesn't, re-check the Web App URL and that "Anyone" access is set.
  • Step 6. To close applications after 30 May: edit apply.html, remove the submit button, and replace it with a "Applications are now closed" message. Re-upload to GitHub.

Apps Script code

// Bridge Program — Google Apps Script // Paste this into Extensions → Apps Script in your Google Sheet // Then deploy as a Web App (Execute as: Me, Access: Anyone) function doPost(e) { const sheet = SpreadsheetApp.getActiveSpreadsheet() .getSheetByName('Responses'); const data = JSON.parse(e.postData.contents); sheet.appendRow([ new Date(), // Timestamp data.name, // Full name data.email, // Work email data.company, // Phiture or Precis data.role, // Job title data.department || '', // Department (optional) data.q1, // Skill to teach (primary) data.q2 || '', // Skill to teach (secondary, optional) data.q3, // Skill to learn data.q4, // Mindset / cross-difference example data.q5, // Hours per month commitment data.q6, // Integration context data.q7 || '' // Precis-only question (blank for Phiture) ]); return ContentService .createTextOutput(JSON.stringify({ status: 'ok' })) .setMimeType(ContentService.MimeType.JSON); } // Optional: add this function to test the sheet connection function testWrite() { const sheet = SpreadsheetApp.getActiveSpreadsheet() .getSheetByName('Responses'); sheet.appendRow(['TEST', 'Test User', 'test@test.com', 'Phiture', 'Tester', '', 'Test Q1', '', 'Test Q3', 'Test Q4', '4h', 'Test Q6', '']); Logger.log('Test row written successfully'); }
GDPR note Application data includes names, emails, and personal reflections. Restrict access to the Google Sheet to the People team only. Do not share the raw Sheet with hiring managers or anyone outside the programme coordination team. Retain data only for the duration of the programme plus 6 months, then delete responses.

Troubleshooting

  • Form submits but no data in the Sheet. Check that "Who has access" is set to "Anyone" (not "Anyone with Google account"). Re-deploy as a new version after changing this setting.
  • CORS error in browser console. Google Apps Script handles CORS automatically for POST requests. If you see a CORS error, check that you are posting to the correct Web App URL (not the editor URL).
  • Sheet tab not found error. Make sure the tab is named exactly "Responses" with a capital R. Apps Script is case-sensitive.
  • Form shows "Something went wrong." The Apps Script may have hit the Google quota limit (rare for small volumes). Wait 30 minutes and try again, or contact Google Workspace support.

Tech setup: Google Apps Script

The application form (apply.html) posts directly to a Google Sheet via Google Apps Script. There is no backend server or database. Setup takes around 10 minutes.

What you need A Google account with access to Google Sheets and Apps Script. The Google Sheet should be in a shared People & Culture Drive folder, not a personal Drive.

Step-by-step setup

  • Step 1. Create a new Google Sheet. Rename the default tab to Responses. In row 1, add these headers exactly (one per column): Timestamp, Name, Email, Company, Role, Department, Q1_Skill_Teach, Q2_Skill_Teach_2, Q3_Skill_Learn, Q4_Mindset, Q5_Hours, Q6_Integration, Q7_Precis_Only
  • Step 2. In the Sheet, go to Extensions → Apps Script. Delete any existing code and paste the script below.
  • Step 3. Click Deploy → New deployment. Choose Web app. Set "Execute as" to Me. Set "Who has access" to Anyone (this allows the form to post without login). Click Deploy. Copy the Web App URL.
  • Step 4. Open apply.html in a text editor. Find the line const SCRIPT_URL = 'YOUR_SCRIPT_URL_HERE'; near the bottom and replace the placeholder with your copied URL. Save and re-upload to GitHub.
  • Step 5. Test: fill in the form and submit. Check the Sheet — a new row should appear with your test data. If it doesn't, re-check the Web App URL and that "Anyone" access is set.
  • Step 6. To close applications after 30 May: edit apply.html, remove the submit button, and replace it with a "Applications are now closed" message. Re-upload to GitHub.

Troubleshooting

  • Form submits but no data in the Sheet. Check that "Who has access" is set to "Anyone" (not "Anyone with Google account"). Re-deploy as a new version after changing this setting.
  • CORS error in browser console. Google Apps Script handles CORS automatically for POST requests. If you see a CORS error, check that you are posting to the correct Web App URL (not the editor URL).
  • Sheet tab not found error. Make sure the tab is named exactly "Responses" with a capital R. Apps Script is case-sensitive.
  • Form shows "Something went wrong." The Apps Script may have hit the Google quota limit (rare for small volumes). Wait 30 minutes and try again, or contact Google Workspace support.
